About Me

My academic interests

My academic interests involve Physical interactions, Affective computing, Ubiquitous computing, and Somaesthetics and the body in HCI. Sustainability and ethics are hugely important to me, both of which are aspects I find sometimes get put on the back burner especially in HCI.
My dream occupation allows me to improve the lives of others through creative and ethical innovations in HCI.

My strengths

The traits what I value the most in myself are my compassion, intelligence, and sense of humor. Even though I have the capacity to stay professional, I find that humor is a great way to nourish creativity, and I tend to not take myself or life too seriously. I am a creative, kind, and open-minded person who works great both in groups and on my own. I thrives from structure, which is why I never go far without my trusty planner. During my studies I have found out I am somewhat of a mean, lean typing machine, as I have received many comments on how quickly I can parse loose findings or open discussions into quality written reports. A strange compliment perhaps, but I take it to heart nonetheless.

Hobbies and Interests

In my spare time I do handcrafts such as sewing and crocheting, tend to my houseplants, and sift through second hand stores in search for interesting items. I like to stay active and spend time in nature, so I lift weights, attend dance classes, and go on long walks in my spare time. In 10 years I see myself sharing my beautiful 1970's house with a large greenhouse with several dogs.
