Projects

On this page I have gathered a few of the projects I have worked on, mostly from my studies. In the gallery below, you'll find some of the bigger projects such as my BSc and MSc thesis projects. Some other examples of my work are the course project on haptic interactions investingating the possibility of using haptics to convey emotions, a paper about the use of HCI technology to reduce loneliness, and an investigation of sustainability in the life cycle of different display technologies.

1 / 7

My Masters thesis in Interactive Media Technology from The Royal Institute of Technology KTH, finished in 2022.
What I did:
In this thesis I investigated relaxation in physical interactions through the method of Soma Design by exploring ASMR media and the bodily experiences of feeling ASMR. The design process involved first person material exploration, somaesthetic sensitivity training in the form of meditation, a workshop with another interaction designer, and data analysis in the form of affinity diagrams. You can click here to download the thesis.
What I learned:

  • Work with a novel design theory when learning about Soma design and implementing its methods and thinking
  • Independently drive and complete a project
  • Iteratively evaluate and decide where to go next during an entire project process
  • Research and gather knowledge in the academic field

2 / 7

My Bachelor thesis in IT engineering at The Royal Institute of Technology KTH, finished in 2020.
What I did:
In this thesis, we produced a tool to aid in the selection of different application technologies. The tool asks a few questions, and based on the results from usability test studies we ran on native applications and progressive web applications, the tool can recommend which technology is most suitable for the planned application. The project was performed with support from a IT consultant company, and during the project we worked in their offices and held interviews with their employees wirking with mobile application development. You can click here to download the thesis.
What I learned:

  • Compare, chose, and work with UX evaluation methods for mobile applications
  • Plan, perform, and evaluate results from usability testing
  • Project completion and scheduling in a team of two
  • Work in an office environment

4 / 7

Project course in Media Technology and Interaction Design
What I did:
My group and I decided to design a potential solution to problems with remote working. The design process included e.g. interviews, persona creation, and low-fi prototyping. It led us to the idea of OnBoard, a device that could provide those working from home with a physical representation of the workspace in order to improve feelings of beloning and control. To view the whole design process, follow this link to the Behance presentation.
What I learned:

  • Perform and evaluate interviews for interaction design projects
  • Create and use personas in a design process
  • Ideation techniques such as brainstorming
  • Teamwork in a remote setting

WorldPong is a concept/prototype of a mobile game.
What I did:
I created this to introduce myself to Figma based on an original idea for an engaging mobile game. The concept revolves around a game where you track WorldPongs, pingpong like digital balls which travel around the world. 7 / 7

Project for the course Advanced Project course in Interactive Media Technology.
What I did:
In the course we developed the "Move it Move it Microwave", a dance-controlled microwave designed to encourage playful interactions in the kitchen environment. The device used an Arduino board and motion detection software to control the switch in the microwave, producing a microwave which only worked when you danced in front of it. In addition, it plays a musical beat if more than one person participates, which invites for social interactions. Click here to download the "Move it Move it Microwave" project report.
What I learned:

  • Ideation methods for physical interactions such as bodystorming
  • Researching and writing papers on highly specific research area
  • Problem solving outside of my regular field of knowledge when "hacking" the microwave
